# 👤 Standing on the Rim — First-Person View

## Prompt

Photorealistic wide-angle photograph taken at eye level (1.7 meters height) inside a massive rotating space habitat cylinder, 982 meters in radius and 2 kilometers long. No human body, hands, or legs visible in frame — this is a clean landscape photograph taken by a camera on a tripod.

The camera stands on a grassy landscape strip approximately 1 kilometer wide. The ground curves noticeably UPWARD to the left and right — the horizon RISES on both sides rather than dropping away. There is no horizon line — the land just keeps curving up and eventually goes overhead.

Small trees, pathways, wildflowers, and low residential buildings (2-3 stories, modern minimal design) line the landscape. A narrow stream runs through the scene. Soil depth is about 1.5 meters over structural hull.

To the left and right, the land strip ends at a visible aluminum rail seam where a transparent window strip begins — a single continuous band of fused quartz glass panels held in a fine diamond-pattern steel cable mesh, approximately 1 kilometer wide. Through the window strips, deep black space with sharp pinpoint white stars is visible — no blue sky, no atmosphere glow. ONE enormous flat polished aluminum mirror per window strip is visible outside, angled at 30 degrees like a giant venetian blind, reflecting harsh white unfiltered sunlight inward. The reflected sunlight creates warm directional illumination with distinct shadows, as if from a low sun.

Beyond each window strip, the next land strip curves further upward, continuing overhead. Looking straight ahead and up, the opposite land strip is visible 1,964 meters directly above — an inverted landscape with tiny buildings, trees, pathways, and green vegetation. The opposite surface mirrors this side — grass, buildings, trees — just upside-down. The distance makes details small but visible, like viewing from a high-altitude aircraft.

The three land strips and three window strips alternate in equal-width bands (each 60 degrees of arc, approximately 1 kilometer wide), wrapping the full 360 degrees of the cylinder interior. The curvature is dramatic and unmistakable — this is clearly an enclosed cylinder, not an open world.

Clear atmosphere with slight haze at distance. Small cumulus clouds float at 200-300 meters altitude. Warm golden-hour lighting.

Style: ultra-realistic landscape photography, NASA concept art, 24mm wide-angle lens on tripod, NO people or body parts in frame, horizontal composition, depth of field with sharp foreground and slightly hazy far side.
