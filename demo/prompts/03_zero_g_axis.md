# 🚀 Zero-G Axis — Floating Weightless at the Center

## Prompt

Photorealistic view floating weightless at the central axis of a rotating cylindrical space habitat. The viewer is suspended in zero gravity at the exact center of a cylinder 1,964 meters in diameter and 2 kilometers long.

Looking outward in any radial direction, the inner surface of the cylinder is 982 meters away — a patchwork of three green landscape strips alternating with three transparent window strips, all curving around the full 360 degrees. The landscape strips show tiny buildings, trees, pathways, and small bodies of water. The window strips glow with sunlight reflected from external mirrors. The entire interior rotates slowly — one full revolution every 62 seconds — creating a mesmerizing panoramic rotation of the world around you.

A thin structural tube (the axial spine) runs the full 2-kilometer length of the cylinder, providing a handhold for movement. Docking port rings are visible at each end — circular structures where spacecraft connect. The end caps are hemispherical gray walls closing off the cylinder at each end, approximately 1 kilometer away in both directions.

Several people float nearby, some with small fabric wings strapped to their arms — human-powered flight is possible in this weightless zone. A spherical water droplet the size of a basketball floats nearby, held together by surface tension. The atmosphere is the same Earth-normal air as at the rim, just weightless.

Radial elevator shafts — thin enclosed tubes — extend from the axis outward to the rim at six evenly-spaced angles, like spokes of a wheel but not structural.

Style: photorealistic, zero-gravity environment, wide-angle fisheye perspective emphasizing the cylindrical wraparound, warm diffuse lighting from all directions, sense of wonder and scale.

## Key Physics Details for Accuracy

- Zero gravity at the axis — objects float, liquids form spheres
- The walls are 982 meters away in every radial direction
- The entire interior surface rotates around you (you co-rotate with the air)
- Air pressure is normal — you can breathe, speak, and fly with wings
- The Coriolis effect is noticeable here — thrown objects curve
- Light comes from the window strips, creating three bright bands and three darker bands
- Looking "down" any radial direction, gravity increases linearly — 0.5g at 491m, 1g at 982m
