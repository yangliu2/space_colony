# Space Portfolio — Abandon & Degrade Criteria

## Purpose
Define clear conditions under which the long-term space industrialization investment thesis should be reduced or abandoned.

---

## 1. Hard Abandon Criteria (Immediate Exit)

### A. Space Not Economically Viable
Trigger if 2–3 of the following occur:
- Launch costs stop decreasing for 10+ years
- No new large-scale space stations after ISS replacement
- Structural reduction in government deep-space budgets

Conclusion:
Space remains science/defense, not industry.

---

### B. No Off-Earth Economy by ~2045
- No sustained lunar operations
- No large-scale in-space manufacturing
- No commercial stations with real revenue

Conclusion:
O’Neill cylinder pathway not viable within lifetime.

---

### C. Dominant Private Monopoly
- A private company (e.g., SpaceX-like) captures most economic value
- Vertical integration blocks public investment exposure

Conclusion:
Public markets are no longer the correct vehicle.

---

### D. Personal Capital Needs
- Education, housing, healthcare pressures
- Career or income instability

Conclusion:
Portfolio risk becomes inappropriate.

---

## 2. Soft Abandon Criteria (Gradual Exit)

### E. Underperformance vs Market
- Underperforms S&P 500 over 10–15 years

Action:
Re-evaluate conviction vs stubbornness.

---

### F. Persistent Dilution
- Repeated share issuance
- Weak or stagnant revenue growth

Action:
Exit affected positions.

---

### G. Narrative Without Delivery
- Frequent industry pivots without execution
- Trend chasing (AI, quantum, etc.) without tangible progress

Action:
Reduce exposure.

---

## 3. Personal Criteria

### H. Loss of Engagement
- No longer tracking developments
- Skipping annual reviews

Action:
Exit or simplify strategy.

---

### I. Time Horizon Shift
- Transition to capital preservation mindset

Action:
Reduce speculative exposure.

---

## 4. Degradation Strategy (Preferred Over Full Exit)

### Level 1 — Full Thesis
- 20% long-shot allocation

### Level 2 — Reduced Confidence
- 10% long-shot allocation

### Level 3 — Weak Thesis
- Exit long shots entirely
- Retain only core (defense/industrial)

### Level 4 — Full Exit
- Transition to broad market index

---

## 5. Anchor Rule

If there is no meaningful off-Earth economy by 2045, exit the thesis.

---

## Key Insight

You don’t lose money by being wrong.

You lose money by being right too early and staying too long.
