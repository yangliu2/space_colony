# ☀️ Mirror Daylight — Sunrise Through the Windows

## Prompt

Photorealistic interior view of an O'Neill cylinder habitat during simulated sunrise. One of the three enormous external mirrors is slowly opening — pivoting from its closed (flat against hull) position to its open (30-degree angle) position, gradually allowing reflected sunlight to stream through the transparent window strip.

The scene captures the transition moment: warm golden light floods through the kilometer-wide window strip at a low angle, casting long dramatic shadows across the landscape. The land strip nearest to the opening mirror is illuminated first — grass, trees, and building facades catch the warm light while the far side of the cylinder remains in pre-dawn dimness.

The mirror itself is visible through the window — an enormous flat polished aluminum surface, easily a kilometer wide and two kilometers long, rotating on its hinge like a giant venetian blind slat. Its surface catches the raw sunlight and redirects it inward. The sun itself is not directly visible — only its reflection in the mirror.

The window strip frames the view: fused quartz panels in thin steel cable mesh, the individual panels visible as a subtle grid pattern. Through the glass, the mirror's edge and the black void of space are visible beyond.

Inside the habitat, the landscape responds to dawn: dew on grass catches the light, birds (imported species) begin to move, street lights along pathways dim automatically. The opposite side of the cylinder (nearly 2 km overhead) is still partially dark — its corresponding mirror hasn't opened yet, creating asymmetric lighting across the interior.

At the cylinder axis, 982 meters up, the atmosphere creates slight haze, softening the view of the opposite land strip. Small clouds from the night's cooling cycle are catching the first light from below, glowing pink and gold.

Style: photorealistic golden hour photography, dramatic directional lighting, lens flare through window panels, warm color palette, cinematic composition with foreground grass/dew detail and background cylinder curvature. The beauty of this scene is the engineered sunrise — nature simulated by mirrors and glass at massive scale.

## Key Physics Details for Accuracy

- Sunrise is MECHANICAL — mirrors physically open on hinges, not a sun rising over a horizon
- The "sun" is a reflection — directional but not a point source (mirror is ~1 km wide)
- Dawn happens simultaneously along the full 2 km length (mirror opens along its full length)
- Different mirrors can open at different rates — the habitat operator controls sunrise timing
- Light entering through one window illuminates parts of all three land strips
- The color temperature is slightly different from Earth sunrise — no atmospheric reddening of raw sunlight, but the mirror angle creates oblique reflection that warms the color
- Weather systems inside the cylinder respond to the heating — convection begins at dawn
- The rotation of the cylinder means the "sunrise direction" changes relative to land features once per minute — shadows sweep around
