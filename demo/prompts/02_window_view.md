# 🪟 Window Strip View — Looking Outward Through Glass

## Prompt

Photorealistic view from inside a rotating space habitat, standing upright on a grassy land strip at the edge where it meets a transparent window strip. The viewer stands at ground level looking sideways through the window — the window strip curves BESIDE the viewer like a wall, not below them. The ground is flat underfoot (grass, soil) and the glass window begins at the seam where the land ends.

The window is a single continuous transparent panel spanning approximately 1 kilometer wide and 2 kilometers long, made of fused quartz glass with a very slight blue-green tint. A fine steel cable mesh — hair-thin wires in a diamond grid pattern — is visible across the glass surface, carrying the structural hoop stress. The cables catch the light slightly, creating a subtle geometric pattern across the view.

Through the window, ONE enormous flat mirror is visible — a single polished aluminum panel approximately 1 kilometer wide and 2 kilometers long, hinged along its bottom edge where it connects to the cylinder hull. The mirror is angled open at roughly 30 degrees from the hull, like a giant venetian blind slat tilted open. It reflects brilliant white unfiltered sunlight inward through the window. The mirror surface is smooth and uniform, not segmented — one continuous reflective sheet the full length of the cylinder.

Beyond the mirror, deep space is visible — sharp pinpoint stars against absolute black. No atmosphere, no glow, no nebulae — just hard vacuum with clean white stars.

The seam between the land strip and window strip shows a structural aluminum rail — a clean edge where soil and grass meet glass and metal. The window curves gently away from the viewer in both directions (curving upward to the left, curving downward to the right) following the cylinder's circumference.

Looking along the length of the cylinder through the window, the mirror stretches to the distant end cap 2 kilometers away. The slight curvature of the cylinder is visible in how the window strip bends.

Style: photorealistic, first-person standing perspective, dramatic contrast between warm grass foreground and cold space through glass, the mirror should dominate the exterior view as an enormous bright reflective surface, cinematic wide-angle composition.

## Key Physics Details for Accuracy

- The viewer stands UPRIGHT on the land strip — the window is BESIDE them curving away, not below
- ONE mirror per window strip, not multiple small mirrors — each mirror is the same size as the window
- The mirror is a single flat sheet, hinged along one long edge at the hull
- Cable mesh is visible across the glass — fine diamond-pattern wires
- Stars don't twinkle (no atmosphere outside)
- Sunlight is unfiltered — harsher and whiter than Earth sunlight
- The window strip curves following the cylinder circumference (60° arc, ~1 km wide)
- Gravity is 1g where the viewer stands — everything feels normal except the view
- There is no horizon — the land curves UP in the distance, not down
