# Space Portfolio (Long-Horizon Strategy)

## 1. Objective
Build a multi-decade portfolio aligned with the long-term thesis of space industrialization (including asteroid mining and large-scale habitats), while prioritizing survival and disciplined exposure to asymmetric upside.

Time Horizon: 20–40 years (intergenerational possible)

---

## 2. Core Principles

1. Survival > Vision
   - Most early companies will fail
   - Value accrues to those still operating when the market matures

2. Infrastructure First
   - Launch, manufacturing, and systems precede biology and habitats

3. Biology is Late-Stage
   - Life-support ecosystems are critical but not near-term monetizable

4. Avoid Overconcentration in Narratives
   - Do not overweight speculative companies without revenue

5. Allocate for Optionality
   - Maintain a controlled % for high-risk, high-reward bets

---

## 3. Portfolio Allocation

### Core (80%) — Survival Layer

- Lockheed Martin — 15%
- Honeywell — 10%
- Teledyne Technologies — 10%

- Rocket Lab — 20%
- Redwire — 10%

- BlackSky — 5%
- Planet Labs — 5%
- Iridium Communications — 5%

---

### Long-Shot (20%) — Asymmetric Upside

- Intuitive Machines — 5%
- AST SpaceMobile — 5%

- Additional allocation (10%) to:
  - Rocket Lab OR
  - Redwire

Rule: Do not diversify excessively within long-shot bucket. Concentration improves payoff probability.

---

## 4. Scenario Expectations

### Scenario A — Space Economy Growth
- Long-shot and infrastructure companies outperform

### Scenario B — Slow Development (Most Likely)
- Defense and industrial companies dominate returns

### Scenario C — Capital Contraction
- Small-cap space companies fail or dilute heavily
- Core allocation preserves capital

### Scenario D — Industry Consolidation
- A dominant private player captures value
- Public mid-tier companies underperform

---

## 5. Rebalancing Rules

### Rule 1 — Annual Review (Mandatory)
- Review once per year
- Do not react to short-term volatility

### Rule 2 — Kill the Walking Dead
Sell if ANY occur:
- No meaningful revenue growth
- Repeated share dilution
- Failed missions without recovery plan
- Constant strategy shifts

---

### Rule 3 — Reward Execution
Increase allocation if:
- Consistent mission success
- Expanding contracts
- Moving up the value chain

Cap: No single company >35%

---

### Rule 4 — No Blind Averaging Down
- Only add if thesis remains intact
- Max add: +25% of original position

---

### Rule 5 — Maintain Structure
- Core: 70–80%
- Long-shot: ~20%

Rebalance every 2–3 years

---

### Rule 6 — Add New Entrants
Every 2–3 years:
- Evaluate new companies
- Replace weaker positions if better candidates emerge

---

## 6. Evaluation Framework (Simple Scoring)

Score each company (0–100) using:

- Revenue growth
- Contract wins
- Technical execution (missions/products)
- Dilution (shares outstanding)

Guideline:
- <40 → consider exit
- 40–70 → hold
- >70 → consider increasing position

---

## 7. Long-Term Thesis Statement

- Space industrialization will occur over decades
- Key bottlenecks:
  - launch cost
  - in-space manufacturing
  - life support systems
- Biology becomes critical only after infrastructure exists
- Survivors capture most value, not early innovators

---

## 8. Mindset

- Ignore noise under 5 years
- Expect most speculative companies to fail
- Expect timeline delays
- Optimize for durability and adaptability

---

## 9. Succession Note (Optional)

If transferred:
- Maintain allocation discipline
- Follow rules before intuition
- Re-evaluate thesis every decade, not annually
