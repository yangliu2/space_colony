# 🌌 Exterior — Full Colony in Space

## Prompt

Photorealistic exterior view of a complete O'Neill cylinder colony floating at the L5 Lagrange point in deep space. Two identical cylinders are connected end-to-end by a bearing structure, counter-rotating to cancel angular momentum.

Each cylinder is 982 meters in radius (nearly 2 km diameter) and 2 kilometers long — roughly the proportions of a large soup can. The cylinders rotate visibly, completing one revolution every 62 seconds. The rotation is smooth and steady.

The near cylinder shows three distinct surface types in alternating 60-degree strips running the full length:
- Three opaque land strips — dark with a slight green-brown tint from the interior surface materials, covered on the outside with thick gray-brown regolith (lunar soil) radiation shielding, 2-5 meters deep
- Three transparent window strips — glowing warmly from interior reflected sunlight, the glass panels catching light in a distinctive way

Three enormous flat mirror panels extend outward from the cylinder, one per window strip. Each mirror is approximately 1 km wide and 2 km long, hinged along one edge at the cylinder hull. They are angled at about 30 degrees, reflecting brilliant unfiltered sunlight through the window strips into the interior. The mirrors are polished aluminum, gleaming white in direct sunlight.

Hemispherical end caps seal each end of the cylinder, with docking port structures visible at the rotation axis. Twelve agriculture pods (six per end) cluster around each end cap — small green-tinted pressurized capsules arranged in a ring.

The bearing connection between the two cylinders shows three structural rods linking the adjacent end caps, allowing counter-rotation. The second cylinder is partially visible, rotating in the opposite direction.

Background: black space with the Milky Way visible, the Sun providing harsh directional lighting from one side, the Earth and Moon as distant blue and gray discs.

Style: photorealistic space visualization, NASA concept art, hard sci-fi aesthetic, dramatic single-source solar lighting with sharp shadows, sense of massive engineering scale. Include a small spacecraft or shuttle nearby for scale reference.

- The exterior is NOT pretty — it's covered in gray regolith shielding
- The only "attractive" exterior elements are the mirrors and the window glow
- Total mass is approximately 46-92 million tonnes
- The regolith shielding is 2-5 tonnes per square meter — meters thick
- Tension cables (wire rope) are visible as fine lines wrapping circumferentially
- Ring rib structures are visible as slight ridges along the length at regular intervals
- The sunlight is unfiltered — harsher and whiter than on Earth's surface
- Counter-rotation means the two cylinders spin in opposite directions
- No exhaust, no engines visible — the colony maintains its position at L5 gravitationally
