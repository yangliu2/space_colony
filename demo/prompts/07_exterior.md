# 🌌 Exterior — Full Colony in Space

## Known Issues with Current Image

The current `exterior.png` has several physics errors that need to be corrected
in the next generation:

1. **Cylinder arrangement is wrong.** The image shows the two cylinders connected
   end-to-end. They should be **side-by-side (parallel)**, separated by a gap,
   connected by a bearing framework spanning between them.
2. **Mirror orientation is wrong.** The image shows mirrors extending from the
   sides of each cylinder like perpendicular fins/wings. Mirrors should extend
   **diagonally from the anti-sun end only**, at 45° from the tangent plane.
   Each mirror is a flexible reflective surface hinged at the center of its
   window strip at the anti-sun end. Three mirrors per cylinder, not six.
3. **Mirror shape is wrong.** The image shows rigid rectangular panels. Mirrors
   are flexible reflective surfaces with stiffener ribs on the back face.
4. **60° stagger missing.** The second cylinder's strip pattern should be
   rotated 60° relative to the first, so the mirrors interleave (don't collide).

## Prompt

Photorealistic exterior view of a complete O'Neill cylinder colony floating at
the L5 Lagrange point in deep space. Two identical cylinders are arranged
**side-by-side (parallel)**, connected by a bearing framework that spans the
gap between them. They counter-rotate to cancel angular momentum.

Each cylinder is 982 meters in radius (nearly 2 km diameter) and 2 kilometers
long — roughly the proportions of a large soup can. The cylinders rotate
visibly, completing one revolution every 62 seconds.

The near cylinder shows three distinct surface types in alternating 60-degree
strips running the full length:
- Three opaque land strips — covered on the outside with thick gray-brown
  regolith (lunar soil) radiation shielding, 2–5 meters deep
- Three transparent window strips — glowing warmly from interior reflected
  sunlight

**Three mirrors extend diagonally from the anti-sun end** of the cylinder (the
end facing away from the sun). Each mirror is a flexible reflective surface,
approximately the width of one window strip and the length of the cylinder.
The mirrors are hinged at the center of each window strip at the anti-sun end
cap, and tilt at 45° from the cylinder surface — extending both outward
(radially) and toward the sun (axially) in a diagonal. The reflective face
(polished aluminum, gleaming white) faces the sun and the window strip below.
The back face (dark thermal coating) faces outward and away from the sun.
The outer edge of each mirror is free (not attached to any structure), held
open by centrifugal force.

The sun-facing end of the cylinder has **no mirrors** — it is a clean
hemispherical end cap with a docking port at the rotation axis.

The second cylinder is side-by-side with the first, separated by a gap (the
gap must be large enough that the mirrors don't collide). Its strip pattern is
**rotated 60°** relative to the first cylinder, so the mirrors of the two
cylinders interleave in the gap rather than colliding.

Hemispherical end caps seal each end, with docking port structures visible at
the rotation axis. Agriculture pods — small green-tinted pressurized capsules
— cluster in a ring around each end cap.

The bearing framework connecting the two cylinders consists of structural
struts spanning between the cylinders at several points along their length.

Background: black space with the Milky Way visible, the Sun providing harsh
directional lighting from one side (along the cylinder axis), Earth and Moon
as distant discs.

Style: photorealistic space visualization, NASA concept art, hard sci-fi
aesthetic, dramatic single-source solar lighting with sharp shadows along
the axis, sense of massive engineering scale. Include a small spacecraft or
shuttle nearby for scale reference.

## Key Physics Details for Accuracy

- The sun is along the cylinder axis — sunlight arrives parallel to the
  long axis, NOT perpendicular to it
- Mirrors are ONLY at the anti-sun end, extending diagonally (not from the
  sides of the cylinder like wings)
- Each mirror's reflective face catches axial sunlight and redirects it 90°
  radially inward through the window strip
- The exterior is mostly covered in gray regolith shielding — not attractive
- Total mass is approximately 46–92 million tonnes
- Tension cables (wire rope) are visible as fine lines wrapping circumferentially
- Counter-rotation means the two cylinders spin in opposite directions
- The two cylinders are PARALLEL (side-by-side), NOT end-to-end
- The gap between cylinders scales with cylinder length (mirrors must not collide)
- No exhaust, no engines visible — the colony maintains position at L5 gravitationally
